Sounds from Tchad Blake

Tune in below to hear a selection of songs by a special guest

SEPTEMBER 07, 2022

T

chad Blake is an American engineer and producer known for his experimental approach to recording and processing sound. His work is immediately recogniseable for its dense low-end and rich harmonics. He has worked with a long list of influential artists, including Tom Waits, Crowded House, Peter Gabriel, The Black Keys, Arctic Monkeys and many more. We sat down with Tchad to find out more about his early influences, as well as the records that have impacted him most over the course of his life and career.

Bob Dylan

The Freewheelin' Bob Dylan

"Blowin' In The Wind"

PROD: JOHN HAMMOND

-- RELEASED 1963

The simplicity of a haiku packed with visions. I never get tired of this song.

Jimi Hendrix

Band Of Gypsys

"Machine Gun"

REC: WALLY HEIDER

REMIX: EDDIE KRAMER

-- RELEASED 1970

The sound and performances in this recording are an emotional rollercoaster. The performance of a lifetime.

King Crimson

Islands

"Ladies Of The Road"

REC: ANDY HENDRIKSON

-- RELEASED 1971

This project is one of the biggest sonic and arrangement influences for me ever. I’ve always loved characterful sounds, a bit out of balance, with lots of contrast. You'll find all of that and more here.

Various

Musique Du Burundi

REC: MICHEL VUYLSTEKE

-- RELEASED 1968

This record blew my mind with sounds and energy. African music has always been very influential for me, it was what basically I started listening to first.

Cardi B

Bodak Yellow

PROD: JWHITE

MIX: EVAN LARAY

-- RELEASED 2019

Here again, simplicity rules. This is one of the coolest vocals I’ve ever heard.

Kendrick Lamar

DAMN.

PROD: VARIOUS

MIX: DEREK ALI

-- RELEASED 2017

Writing, engineering, production and performance all come together in a masterclass of album craft.

Missy "Misdemeanor" Elliott

Supa Dupa Fly

PROD: MISSY ELLIOTT & TIMBALAND

MIX: JIMMY DOUGLASS

-- RELEASED 1997

A perfect album.

Stevie Wonder

Fulfillingness' First Finale

"You Haven't Done Nothin'"

REC: GARY OLAZABAL & JOAN DECOLA

-- RELEASED 1974

I couldn't leave this one out. No words can convey what this song means to me.

Exploring Tchad's Work

In addition to discussing Tchad's favourite songs, we also asked him to select a handful of songs from his own discography that have been particularly meaningful to him and his musical journey...

Lisa Germano

Slide

-- RELEASED 1998

I think this is my best work overall. I have tears and smiles to this day when I listen to it and my whole family is just as enamored with it as I am. I love her musical body language and her lyrics…. pure emotion for me. I don’t know anyone who puts so much into their music emotionally.

Ron Sexsmith

Ron Sexsmith

"Words We Never Use"

PROD: MITCHELL FROOM

-- RELEASED 1995

I love the mix of this which wouldn’t have been possible without Ron's beautifully austere writing and Mitchell Froom’s incredible production which lets each player really speak.

Soul Coughing

El Oso

"Rolling"

-- RELEASED 1998

This album is one of my favourites. Their albums Ruby Vroom (1994) & El Oso continue to garner me a few sonic boffin fans, even after all these years.

Los Lobos

Colossal Head

-- RELEASED 1996

It’s a party….what more can I say?

The Black Keys

Brothers

-- RELEASED 2010

I love this band and their music. This record is just me playing around while being egged on by the band. There is real joy and you can hear it. They pushed me hard on this album which I’ll aways be grateful for. I had a good feeling about Brothers when we finished and was chuffed it did so well.

Ed Harcourt

From Every Sphere

"Ghost Writer"

-- RELEASED 2003

Ed is a crazy good songsmith, and what he does is bent in the coolest ways.

Queen Kwong

Couples Only

"I Know Who You Are" and "Biggest Mistake"

-- RELEASED 2022

Just wow. This project came to me from a producer named John Congleton, who I had done some work for a few years back. He recommended me for the project, for which I will be eternally grateful. Thank you, John!

The way she works, from what I understand, is that she goes in with the band and they improvise - including the lyrics! I guess they must set some parameters before, or perhaps they go in and make it a little bit more of a 'song' after, but there is a huge portion of it which is improvised and that just feels different. I loved working on it.

Todd Snider

The Agnostic Church Of Hope And Wonder

"The Get Together"

-- RELEASED 2021

Todd is a great writer from Nashville. He has the coolest vibe... and he let me play a few instruments on several songs!

Nick Harper

Phantastes

"The Moment Came"

-- RELEASED 2022

Nick Harper is a wildly experimental guitar maestro and wordsmith. The record is transporting.

Luke Sital Singh

Dressing Like A Stranger

"Rather Be", "Dressing Like A Stranger" and "Wiser Too"

-- FORTHCOMING

Luke is an incredible multi-instrumenalist with a beautiful voice.
